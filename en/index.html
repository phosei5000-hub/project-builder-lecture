How to plan your Don Quijote shopping run

Don Quijote (ドン・キホーテ) is a chain of large discount variety stores found throughout Japan's major tourist areas — Shinjuku, Shibuya, Osaka's Dotonbori, and more — and most locations stay open late into the night or around the clock. Because the stores are huge and stock everything from medicine to appliances to costumes, first-time visitors can easily lose a big chunk of their trip just hunting for what they came for. Checking this ranking ahead of time and building a shopping list lets you shop efficiently even on a short itinerary.

How to get the tax-free discount

Foreign tourists who present a passport can get the 10% consumption tax waived once their combined purchases at one store total 5,000 yen or more (before tax) in a single day. This is handled at a dedicated tax-free counter where you show your passport. General goods (electronics, general merchandise) and consumables (cosmetics, food, medicine, alcohol, etc.) have different combined limits and packaging rules, so it's safest to confirm with staff before checkout. Consumables are sealed in special tax-free packaging, and opening that seal before you leave Japan can void the exemption — so leave it sealed until you're home.

What to know, category by category

Medicine & Healthcare

The most popular category, since many of these formulas and ingredients aren't sold back home. If you're heading somewhere with restrictions on certain ingredients, check the label before you fly.

🧴

Beauty & Skincare

Steady-sellers from Japanese drugstores and roadside shops all in one place. Patch-test new products on your wrist before putting them straight on your face.

🍫

Snacks & Sweets

The easiest, lowest-pressure gift category. Chocolate doesn't do well in summer heat, so pack an insulated pouch if you're carrying it around during the day.

🍜

Food, Sauces & Alcohol

Check your home country's duty-free alcohol allowance before you buy multiple bottles, and pack liquids in checked luggage — carrying them on can mean they get confiscated at security.

🛌

Lifestyle & Self-Care

Leg and eye fatigue relief is especially popular given how much walking a Japan trip involves. These work best used your first night at the hotel or on the flight home.

Frequently asked questions

What time is Don Quijote open until?
Most city-center stores are open 24 hours or until 2–5am, but hours vary by location, so it's worth checking the specific store's hours before you go.
Can I pay by credit card or mobile payment?
Most stores accept foreign-issued credit cards, IC transit cards, and various QR payment apps. A few smaller locations may have limited payment options, so it's worth carrying some cash as a backup.
Is this an official Don Quijote site?
No. This is an independent, unofficial reference site with no affiliation or sponsorship from Don Quijote or any related brand. It organizes rankings and prices purely for travelers' reference.
The price doesn't match what I saw in-store — why?
Prices here are reference figures based on Amazon Japan's retail price. Exchange rates, store location, and promotions can all change the in-store price, so please confirm at the register before buying.
